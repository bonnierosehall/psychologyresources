Art Therapy Practices

Art therapy uses creative expression as a pathway to healing, insight, and personal growth. It’s not about artistic skill — it’s about emotional honesty, process, and reflection.

Benefits of Art Therapy

  • 🌿 Helps express what words cannot say
  • 🖼️ Externalizes thoughts and emotions
  • 🧠 Engages different parts of the brain for healing
  • 🧘‍♀️ Provides mindfulness and grounding
  • 💬 Opens up conversation and insight in therapy

Gentle Art Therapy Practices

1. Visual Journaling 🖋️

Use mixed media (markers, collage, watercolor) to capture thoughts, moods, or events of the day. Try including both images and text.

2. Safe Space Drawing 🏡

Draw or paint a “safe place” — real or imagined — where you feel calm and protected. Revisit it when you're feeling overwhelmed.

3. Scribble and See 🌀

Make random scribbles, then find shapes or figures within them. Let your imagination lead. This can release tension and bypass overthinking.

4. Emotion Wheels 🎨

Use color to represent emotions. Create a wheel or gradient to explore how different feelings interact or change over time.

5. Letter to Your Younger Self ✉️

Create an illustrated letter or collage addressed to your inner child. Include words of encouragement, comfort, and connection.

“Art enables us to find ourselves and lose ourselves at the same time.” — Thomas Merton

Tips for Getting Started

  • 🎧 Play soft instrumental music or sit in silence
  • ⏳ Set aside 15–30 minutes — no rushing
  • 🌙 Work under soft lighting for a calming mood
  • 📦 Keep a small kit of art supplies easily accessible
  • 💭 Reflect on your creation — or just let it be
