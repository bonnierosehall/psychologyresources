Biography

Abraham Harold Maslow (1908â€“1970) was an American psychologist best known for creating Maslow's hierarchy of needs, a theory of psychological health based on fulfilling innate human needs in priority, culminating in self-actualization.

Born in Brooklyn, New York, to Russian-Jewish immigrants, Maslow began his career interested in behaviorism but soon shifted toward understanding the full potential of the human spirit. He believed that people are motivated by more than just survival and that creativity, love, and meaning are essential to human flourishing.

His work inspired generations of psychologists, educators, and counselors, and he remains a cherished figure in the world of personal growth and wellness.
