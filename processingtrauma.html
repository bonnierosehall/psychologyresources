This could be through journaling, therapy, art, or trusted conversations. You decide what, when, and how much to share. Maybe you simply don't know anyone eho is going to be supportive and give you the validation that you need. Try writing it down in its ugly truth and see if you you can validate it yourself. "Yes, that really did happen and it happened exactly the way I said it did." Burn the paper if you need to. Rip it to shreds if you need to. But write it down. This might feel really awkward at first. If you just write "fuck you" on the page a thousand times, job done. Try again the next day and see if you don't have more of a coherent narrative this time round. If this feels impossible, start by drawing a picture. Any picture. You might have the disconcerting experience of drawing a something warm and fuzzy like a bunny rabbit and then completely destroying it in red or black. This is totally normal, you're not going crazy. Chuck it out if it makes you feel bad and try again when you're ready. If you have company, awesome, but if you don't, just be your own company. Try and notice, really notice what's around you. If you're in an urban environment, notice the shops - are they boarded up? Are they looking for new employees? Does the local hair salon have some weird doggy/owner spa day thing being advertised? Just try and notice what's going on around you.

The reality is that it is your story, even if you really, really wish it wasn't. This is the part where you can say "wow, there is something really wrong with those people. Why would they behave like that?" and you might even find yourself feeling kind of sorry for them that they don't know any better.
