Carl Rogers was a pioneering American psychologist and one of the founders of the humanistic approach to psychology. He is best known for his theory of client-centered therapy, emphasizing empathy, genuineness, and unconditional positive regard.

Rogers believed in the inherent worth of every individual and the importance of creating a safe therapeutic environment in which a person can grow and self-actualize.

Legacy

Carl Rogers influenced not only psychotherapy but also education, healthcare, conflict resolution, and more. His deep respect for the individual and his trust in the human capacity for growth continue to inspire therapists, educators, and change-makers around the world.
