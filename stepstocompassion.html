Steps to Compassion

1. Awareness

“You must first see a person before you can love them.” — Cornel West

Notice suffering—in yourself or others. Often, we miss this step because we’re distracted or overwhelmed. Begin by slowing down and simply observing.

2. Acknowledgment

“This hurts. This matters.”

Let the suffering be real without trying to fix or avoid it. Just naming it can be a powerful act of compassion.

3. Perspective-Taking (Empathy)

“That could be me. Maybe it is me.”

Try to step into the other person's shoes. Compassion deepens when we see our shared humanity.

4. Intention

“I want to help.”

True compassion arises when we wish to ease another’s suffering—not to control or fix, but to be present with care.

5. Action

“How can I show up with care?”

Compassionate action might be listening, helping, or simply holding space. It’s guided by what’s truly helpful.

6. Sustain with Boundaries & Self-Compassion

“Compassion without boundaries is burnout.” — Brené Brown (paraphrased)

You matter too. Compassion becomes sustainable when you include yourself in its circle.

Spiritual Reflection

Many traditions see compassion as sacred—a reflection of divine love or spiritual unity. Let it flow through you, not just from you.
